03 — How I Think

My edge isn't
just code.

01
Validate before modeling

Two years teaching chemistry means I never skip the hypothesis. I question the data first, understand the domain second, and build the model third. Most errors are upstream — not in the algorithm.

// Scientific rigor
02
Explain, not just predict

A model that stakeholders do not trust does not get used. I default to SHAP, feature importance, and plain-language summaries alongside every metric. Accuracy without interpretability is incomplete.

// Stakeholder-first
03
Biology taught me to question everything

Living systems are messy, context-dependent, and rarely linear — exactly like real-world data. My biology background makes me comfortable with ambiguity, outliers, and shifting baselines that break standard assumptions.

// Domain transfer
